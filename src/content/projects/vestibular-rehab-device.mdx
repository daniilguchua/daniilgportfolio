## Overview

Designed and prototyped a vestibular rehabilitation therapy device in collaboration with clinicians at Shirley Ryan AbilityLab, one of the top rehabilitation hospitals in the United States.

## The Problem

Existing vestibular rehabilitation exercises are difficult for patients to perform correctly at home, leading to poor adherence and slower recovery. Clinicians needed a device that could guide patients through exercises with real-time feedback.

## Design Process

- **User research**: Interviews with physical therapists and patients to understand pain points
- **Rapid prototyping**: Multiple iterations using CAD modeling and 3D printing
- **Clinical feedback**: Regular design reviews with rehabilitation specialists
- **Human factors**: Accessibility considerations for elderly and disabled users

## Impact

The device was presented to clinicians at Shirley Ryan AbilityLab and received positive feedback for its intuitive design and potential to improve patient compliance with home exercise programs.
